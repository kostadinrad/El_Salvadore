El Salvador – Lotería Nacional de Beneficencia (LNB) Gambling Market & Entry Strategy

El Salvador is a compact Central American jurisdiction with approximately 6.37–6.56 million inhabitants in 2025 (projected to reach 6.6 million by 2026). Following the 2021 legislative reform, the Lotería Nacional de Beneficencia (LNB) became the single national authority for lotteries, casinos, sports betting, and online gambling, issuing licenses valid for up to ten years.

The total gambling market is projected at US$381.67 million in 2025, growing to US$412.08 million by 2029 (CAGR ≈1.93%). This represents a significant opportunity for combined online sportsbook and e-lottery operations.

Licensing: Steps to Obtain an LNB Gambling Licence

Step 1 – Local Company Formation

  • Incorporate as an LLC or Corporation and reserve the name with authorities.
  • Obtain Tax ID (NIT) and VAT (IVA) registration from the Ministry of Finance.
  • Open a local corporate bank account for operational and player funds.

Step 2 – Application Preparation

  • Corporate: Articles of incorporation, UBO and director ID, and bank references.
  • Business Plan: Product suite, 10-year financial projections, and investment scale.
  • Compliance: AML/CTF policies, MLRO appointment, and Responsible Gambling (RG) frameworks.
  • Technical: Platform architecture, RNG certificates, and disaster-recovery plans.

Step 3 – Submission and Regulatory Review

  • Submit complete dossier (digital/physical) to LNB.
  • Compliance review: background checks, integrity assessment, and technical evaluation.

Step 4 – Approval and Canon Payment

  • Receive Board decision. If approved, pay the Canon (License Fee) based on term and investment.
  • Licence becomes effective upon payment and fulfillment of specific conditions.

Payment Ecosystem (Multi-Rail Strategy)

Operational success requires catering to both the banked and unbanked populations using El Salvador's unique dual-currency infrastructure.

  • BTC / Chivo Bitcoin Integration: Native support for Bitcoin (legal tender) via Chivo Wallet or Lightning Network.
  • Retail LNB Agent Network: Cash-to-digital top-ups via physical lottery kiosks for the unbanked.
  • Banking Transfer365: 24/7 instant interbank transfers for digital withdrawals and deposits.
  • Cards Visa/Mastercard: Standard gateway for urban, banked demographics.

  • Bet365: Dominant offshore player accessible via international licenses.

Marketing and Advertising Restrictions

  • Licensing: Only LNB-licensed operators can advertise; unlicensed activities face closure.
  • Minors: Strict ban on marketing to under-18s; use age-gated channels.
  • Responsible Gaming: Ads must include disclaimers and must not glamorize gambling as a financial solution.
  • Crypto: Limits on crypto-centric marketing despite BTC legal tender status.
  • Implication: Prioritize KYC-validated channels (CRM/Apps) over broad mass-media bursts.
